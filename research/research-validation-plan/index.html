    Research Validation Plan¶

    This page records what must be validated before the wiki should move from research into solution design.

    The aim is to prevent the project from becoming overconfident. Each workstream should identify who can confirm or challenge the assumption, what evidence to ask for, and what would change the research conclusion.

    Validation groups¶

    Group What to validate Evidence to request Output
    Head of Medical Whether the minimum pack, RFL clause map, concussion controls, medicines controls, and contractor evidence match real club workflow. Current medical governance calendar, anonymised examples of records/checklists, pain points, current tools. Validated or revised core pack and RFL evidence map.
    Club board/CEO What assurance directors need and what clinical detail they should not see. Board paper examples, risk appetite, committee structure, decision thresholds. Board-visible evidence boundary.
    Club operations/match manager Venue, EAP, medical-room, equipment, ambulance access, contractor command-and-control, and fixture logistics. EAP, venue checklist, match-day briefing, contractor plan, venue-change process. Operational evidence checklist.
    Safeguarding/welfare lead Academy, under-18, welfare, mental health, disclosure, and information-sharing boundaries. Safeguarding policy, welfare escalation route, academy consent model, case-access rules. Safeguarding/clinical record separation.
    RFL or league medical source Competition-specific requirements, mandatory/best-practice status, reporting deadlines, and evidence expectations. Current RFL Medical Standards clarification, competition table confirmation, reporting templates. Hardened Championship-specific register.
    CQC/regulatory lawyer CQC scope decision logic, sports exclusions, contractor/control, diagnostics, remote advice, public clinics, and registration risk. Scenario matrix, question bank, service descriptions, contracts. Validated scope decision research and stop/advice triggers.
    Vendors Whether existing systems can export the evidence required and enforce permission boundaries. Demo using scenario evidence, export samples, API docs, access-control model, audit logs. Tool capability comparison evidence.
    Information governance adviser Health data, special category data, controller/processor roles, board reporting, retention, subject access, and sharing. Data flows, privacy notices, contracts, access matrix, retention schedule. IG risk register and access model.

    Validation questions by item¶

    Roadmap item Validation questions
    Item 2: Minimum compliance pack Which components are truly minimum? Which are scenario appendices? Which evidence is board-visible? Which evidence must remain clinical/restricted?
    Item 3: RFL clause map Which clauses are mandatory, best practice, information-only, or competition-specific for Championship? What evidence would RFL actually expect?
    Item 5: CQC scope question bank Which questions are legally decisive? Which facts require stop-and-seek-advice? Which outcomes should never be automated?
    Item 6: Tool landscape Which current tools are actually used by clubs? Which can export usable evidence? Which create clinical safety, IG, or medical-device concerns?

    Validation artefacts to collect¶

    • Anonymised current club medical governance pack.
    • Current fixture/training cover rota and medical staff evidence list.
    • Example EAP and pre-match briefing record.
    • Example contractor contract or service specification for cardiac screening or event medical cover.
    • Example concussion pathway evidence pack.
    • Example board medical assurance report or board risk paper.
    • Vendor export sample for athlete clinical records and compliance evidence.
    • CQC/legal review note on the six scenario matrix cases.

    Completion criteria before solution design¶

    Research should not move into solution design until:

    • the six scenario matrix cases have been validated by at least one Head of Medical or equivalent;
    • the RFL status register has been checked for Championship-specific requirements;
    • the CQC question bank has been reviewed by a regulatory specialist;
    • the board-visible/restricted-clinical evidence boundary has been agreed;
    • at least two vendors or current club systems have been tested against the evidence export questions;
    • unresolved assumptions are listed openly on the relevant page.
