# RFL Medical Standards Map

## Source

This page maps the **RFL Medical Standards 2026** to CQC-style assurance. The standards are part of the RFL Operational Rules and are mandatory where marked as mandatory.

The 2026 standards expressly say they should be applied in accordance with UK healthcare legislation, professional regulator guidance, recognised professional bodies, best practice, and clinical governance standards such as CQC. That makes a CQC-style governance map appropriate even where the club is outside CQC registration.

## RFL structure and CQC alignment

## Mandatory governance controls to evidence

### Medical personnel

Club board assurance should evidence:

- every clinician is registered with the RFL where required;
- doctors, physiotherapists, paramedics, sports rehabilitators, and sports therapists operate within scope of practice;
- IMMOFP or accepted equivalent qualifications are current;
- locums and second medics receive induction and reporting instructions;
- staff are protected from inappropriate pressure over medical decisions;
- match-day and training cover is planned before fixtures are accepted.

### Medical records and information

The 2026 standards require clear, contemporaneous medical records, preferably electronic, encrypted, and secure. Records must be kept for any player receiving medical care or advice, including ad-hoc match cover.

Governance evidence:

- named clinical record system;
- access-control matrix;
- retention schedule;
- locum handover process;
- consent and confidentiality policy;
- loan-player information-sharing protocol;
- audit of record completeness.

### Emergency action planning

Every club must have written medical emergency action plans for matches and training, covering grounds and training facilities used by the club.

Evidence:

- site-specific emergency action plans;
- ambulance access details and venue postcode/access points;
- communication method with emergency services;
- treatment-room and emergency department details;
- next-of-kin notification arrangements;
- arrangements for concussed players not driving home;
- documented sharing with opposition medical staff;
- pre-match briefing record.

### Equipment and facilities

RFL requires mandatory medical equipment and a mandatory drugs box at games and training, with home and away club obligations. Treatment rooms must meet defined standards including access for stretchers, handwashing, cleanable surfaces, sharps/clinical waste, and internet access for note keeping.

Evidence:

- equipment inventory and expiry checks;
- oxygen and AED checks;
- mandatory drugs box expiry and stock checks;
- equipment failure log;
- treatment-room checklist;
- clinical waste disposal contract/process;
- infection-prevention audit.

### Concussion

The RFL standards require annual baseline SCAT6 and Cognigram before contact activity, recognise-and-remove controls, HIA processes, GRTP, reporting within defined timescales, return-to-play forms, and specialist review in defined circumstances.

Evidence:

- valid baseline register before contact training;
- HIA/concussion reports within 24 hours;
- parent-club notification within 12 hours where only the home doctor is present or the player is on loan;
- GRTP oversight by a doctor;
- doctor confirmation before progression to higher-risk stages;
- return-to-play form submitted before match participation;
- post-injury Cognigram file;
- specialist reports and player/parent acknowledgment where required;
- audit of missed, late, or non-compliant concussion steps.

### Cardiac screening

The 2026 standards make cardiac screening mandatory for Super League, Women's Super League, and Championship clubs. This creates both clinical governance and potential diagnostics/provider-control questions.

Evidence:

- annual screening plan;
- provider contract and registration/exemption analysis;
- attendance register;
- abnormal result escalation pathway;
- player communication and records;
- transfer/loan communication where relevant;
- board-level assurance that screening is complete before relevant participation.

## Gap from RFL rules to CQC evidence

The RFL standards are strong sport-specific controls. The CQC-style additions are:

- explicit legal entity accountability;
- provider/control assessment for each healthcare service;
- statement of purpose discipline;
- complaints and duty-of-candour process where regulated care is provided;
- medicines governance beyond match-day minimum kit;
- contractor quality assurance;
- board risk register and formal closure of audit actions;
- equality and player-experience evidence;
- Freedom to Speak Up route for clinicians and players.
