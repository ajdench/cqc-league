    Minimum Compliance Pack Research¶

    This page records the research basis for a minimum compliance pack. It is not yet a final implementation pack and should not be treated as a product specification.

    The research question is: what evidence would a professional Rugby League club reasonably need to hold to show that its medical service is governed, whether or not CQC registration is required?

    Use this page with Assurance Pack Templates, Registration Decision Model, RFL Clause Evidence Map, and CQC Assurance Domains and Quality Statements.

    Research boundary¶

    The pack should support three situations:

    1. the club concludes that the service is outside CQC registration or excluded because it is sports-ground/event treatment for participants or attendees;
    2. the club uses an external provider and needs evidence that the provider, rather than the club, carries on any regulated activity;
    3. the club identifies possible registration risk and needs a controlled evidence pack before taking legal/CQC advice.

    The pack should not assume that every club must register with CQC. It should instead preserve the facts, evidence, and reasoning used to reach the conclusion.

    Source anchors¶

    Source Research implication
    CQC scope of registration The pack must answer activity, provider/control, exception, location, and registration route questions.
    CQC treatment of disease, disorder or injury Sports-ground/event treatment exclusions are central, but wider clinics, diagnostics, prescribing, IV services, public access, or non-sport services can change the analysis.
    CQC evidence categories Evidence should be organised by people's experience, staff/leaders, partners, observation, processes, and outcomes.
    RFL Medical Standards 2026 RFL medical requirements create the sport-specific evidence layer for staff, EAP, equipment, records, anti-doping, concussion, cardiac screening, and policies.
    CQC Regulation 17: Good governance Even where registration is not required, governance evidence should show risk management, audit, records, and action closure.

    Candidate pack inventory¶

    Pack component Research purpose Current evidence hypothesis Validation still needed
    CQC scope decision record Preserve why the club thinks registration is or is not required. Should capture service model, user group, activities, clinicians, exclusions, provider/control, location, reviewer, date, and triggers for review. Test with regulatory counsel and at least one real Championship club service model.
    Medical service description Define what the club medical service is and is not. Should resemble a statement-of-purpose discipline even where CQC registration is not required. Confirm board and Head of Medical language that is usable without implying registered-provider status.
    RFL standards evidence dashboard Show whether sport-specific obligations are evidenced. Should map to A1-F11 in RFL Clause Evidence Map. Confirm Championship-specific requirements and distinguish mandatory, best practice, and information-only clauses.
    Clinical governance meeting record Show medical oversight, decisions, actions, and escalation. Should record incidents, audit results, records, confidentiality, equipment, EAP, concussion, medicines, contractors, and speaking-up issues. Validate cadence and attendance with club medical leads.
    Contractor due diligence file Show who carries on any healthcare activity and what assurance the club receives. Should include registration/exemption analysis, contracts, credentials, incident reporting, audit rights, records, medicines, and quality reports. Test against cardiac screening, ambulance/event cover, diagnostics, and external doctor models.
    Incident and serious injury record Preserve response, notifications, learning, and governance closure. Should include RFL reporting, CQC notification/duty-of-candour review where relevant, safeguarding, anti-doping/TUE flags, and action closure. Align terminology with RFL serious injury/death reporting and club safeguarding process.
    Concussion assurance record Evidence baseline, recognise-and-remove, HIA, GRTP, reporting, and RTP controls. Should use RFL Section E as the source control set. Confirm competition-specific timing and submission evidence for Championship context.
    Medicines and anti-doping log Connect clinical medicines decisions to UKAD/TUE controls. Should record prescriber, medicine, indication, route, dose, consent, allergy, Global DRO/UKAD check, TUE status, batch/expiry where relevant, and follow-up. Review with prescribing clinician and anti-doping lead.
    Board medical assurance report Give directors assurance without exposing confidential clinical detail. Should summarise scope status, RFL compliance, incidents, audit, overdue risks, contractor assurance, and decisions needed. Define what board can see, what Head of Medical retains, and what needs anonymisation.
    Annual medical governance review Close the loop across the season. Should review CQC scope, RFL compliance, incidents, records, medicines, concussion, cardiac screening, contractors, facilities, player experience, and action plans. Test against a club board calendar and season structure.

    Evidence categories to preserve¶

    The pack should deliberately avoid relying only on policies. For each component, research should identify evidence across CQC's categories:

    Evidence category Sports medical examples
    People's experience player feedback, consent concerns, confidentiality concerns, complaint themes, post-concussion welfare experience.
    Staff and leaders clinician feedback, Head of Medical assurance, board minutes, speaking-up issues, locum debriefs.
    Partners RFL, UKAD, ambulance provider, hospital, external screening provider, academy/schools, parent club for loan players.
    Observation treatment-room audit, equipment checks, match-day briefing observation, EAP rehearsal.
    Processes policies, SOPs, scope decision records, incident workflows, medicine checks, GRTP pathway.
    Outcomes concussion compliance, equipment exceptions, record completeness, incident closure, screening completion, overdue actions.

    Research-complete definition¶

    Item 2 should only be marked research-complete when:

    • every candidate pack component has a source basis;
    • each component has a clear evidence owner and review cadence;
    • confidential clinical data and board-visible assurance have been separated;
    • the pack has been tested against at least three scenarios: player-only medical service, external cardiac screening, and public/private clinic risk;
    • regulatory/legal review points are marked clearly rather than implied.

    Open questions¶

    • Which pack components are genuinely minimum for a Championship club, and which are best-practice extras?
    • How much evidence does the board need to hold versus receive as anonymised assurance?
    • Should academy/scholarship arrangements use the same pack or a child-specific variant?
    • How should the pack deal with clubs that already use sports EMR, safeguarding, HR, or incident systems?
    • What evidence would a CQC assessor, RFL reviewer, insurer, or claimant's lawyer actually ask for first?
