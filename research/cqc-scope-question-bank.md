# CQC Scope Question Bank

This page hardens item 5 by converting the [CQC Scope Decision Research](cqc-scope-decision-research.md) into a neutral question bank. It is not a decision wizard. It is a research tool for collecting facts before any conclusion is reached.

The language should remain cautious: "appears", "likely", "needs review", and "seek advice" are safer than "CQC compliant".

## Question bank

| Question | Evidence to collect | Stop or seek advice trigger |
| --- | --- | --- |
| What service is being assessed? | Service description, owner, locations, users, activities, clinicians, records, referrals, payment route. | The service is not clearly described or has changed since the last assessment. |
| Who is the service for? | Eligibility criteria, booking route, website copy, contracts, player/academy/staff/public distinction. | Staff families, sponsors, schools, community users, or public patients are included. |
| Is the activity treatment of disease, disorder or injury? | Clinical pathway, professional roles, supervision, treatment records, medicines/procedures. | Doctor-led treatment, prescribing, injections, IV treatment, or non-player treatment is included. |
| Is the activity diagnostic or screening? | Screening plan, ECG/echo/ultrasound/MRI/blood-test pathway, provider reports, referral rules. | The club controls testing, analysis, reporting, or clinical follow-up. |
| Is there remote advice, triage, or prioritisation? | App/hotline description, response-time promise, triage script, escalation pathway, user group. | The body is established to provide urgent advice, triage, or immediate prioritisation. |
| Is there ambulance transport from the event ground to hospital? | Contractor service spec, vehicle use, transfer records, CQC provider profile. | Contractor or club transports patients to hospital or another healthcare facility. |
| Is the sports-ground/event exclusion being relied on? | Venue/event evidence, participant/attendee status, temporary event plan, associated-premises rationale. | Service is not solely for participants/attendees or is detached from sports premises/events. |
| Who directs and controls clinical provision? | Contracts, governance chart, policies, records ownership, incident route, complaints route, medicines ownership. | Club and contractor share control without clear allocation. |
| Who owns records and data-controller decisions? | Record-system contract, privacy notices, access matrix, sharing agreement, retention rules. | Club has access to full clinical notes without clear role and lawful basis. |
| How are complaints, incidents, serious injuries, safeguarding, and duty-of-candour reviews handled? | Policies, reporting routes, serious incident log, contractor escalation, board reports. | No clear route exists or registered provider responsibilities are unclear. |
| What locations are used? | Stadium, training ground, academy site, third-party clinic, mobile unit, remote administrative base, away venue. | Care is delivered from a public clinic, third-party premises, or remote service base. |
| What would trigger reassessment? | Change log, board approvals, new service proposals, new providers, new activities, new user groups. | Any new diagnostics, public access, remote triage, prescribing/injections/IV, ambulance transport, or contractor change. |

## Scenario test

| Scenario | Questions most likely to drive conclusion | Likely evidence burden |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Player-only match/training medical service | user group; sports exclusion; provider/control; records; RFL evidence. | Moderate: evidence participant-only scope, premises/event link, governance, RFL controls, and no public clinic drift. |
| External cardiac screening provider | diagnostic/screening; contractor status; provider/control; records; abnormal-result pathway. | High: provider registration/exemption, contract, clinical control, completion dashboard, and results governance. |
| Public/private clinic or paid treatment | user group; treatment; payment route; premises; provider/control; registration route. | Very high: stop before launch and seek specialist CQC/legal advice. |
| Remote triage/advice | remote advice; user group; triage/prioritisation; records; safeguarding/parental consent. | High: distinguish follow-up from formal triage service and document escalation/safety-netting. |
| Concussion and return-to-play | sports exclusion; RFL Section E; records; information sharing; medication masking; loan-player handover. | Moderate to high clinical governance burden, lower registration risk where event/participant-only facts hold. |
| Contractor-led ambulance/event medical cover | contractor status; transport; command/control; incident route; records; event exclusion. | High: contract, CQC profile/exemption, command structure, EAP, post-event report, and transport-to-hospital boundary. |

## Minimum outcome evidence

| Outcome label | Questions that must be answered | Evidence threshold |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Outside CQC scope | service description; activity; professional involvement. | Clear evidence no regulated activity is being carried on. |
| Sports/event exclusion likely applies | user group; venue/event; treatment activity; exclusion evidence. | Evidence that care is solely for participants/attendees at sports premises/events. |
| Contractor-led | provider/control; contractor status; records; incident route. | Contract and provider evidence show contractor carries on and governs the relevant activity. |
| Mixed or borderline | any unclear question above. | Gap list, risk rating, ring-fence/pause recommendation, advice record. |
| Potential registration risk | regulated activity; no clear exclusion; club control; location. | Full registration/legal advice pack before launch or continuation. |
