    RFL Clause Evidence Map¶

    This page is a first-pass research map from the RFL Medical Standards 2026 into CQC-style evidence. It sits beneath the higher-level RFL Medical Standards Map. For status classification, Championship relevance, and board-visible versus restricted evidence, use RFL Clause Status Register.

    The purpose is not to reproduce the RFL standards or to finalise software requirements. It is to identify what a club may need to evidence, and what still needs validation, if asked, "How do you know this medical service is governed?"

    Research maturity¶

    Area Current maturity What still needs doing
    Clause coverage First-pass A1-F11 coverage is in place. Check every clause against the RFL PDF and add missing subclauses where they materially change evidence.
    Mandatory status Mandatory language is clear for many areas, but not all rows are yet classified. Add a mandatory / best practice / information-only / competition-specific status column after validation.
    Competition specificity Championship is referenced where obvious, for example cardiac screening and equipment. Confirm differences for Super League, Championship, WSL, academy, reserves, scholarship, community, and away/travel contexts.
    Evidence ownership Indicative owners are listed. Validate with a Head of Medical, club operations lead, welfare/safeguarding lead, and external provider.
    CQC mapping High-level CQC lens is included. Cross-check against CQC evidence categories and quality statements once the evidence pack is stabilised.

    Status triage to validate¶

    RFL area Apparent status from source Validation note
    A1 medical staff registration Mandatory. Confirm role-by-role requirement and club evidence route.
    A3 prehospital trauma courses Mandatory for field-of-play personnel. Confirm acceptable equivalents, grace periods, and competition differences.
    A4 attendance at matches and training Mandatory. Confirm exact cover requirement for Championship home, away, training, academy, and reserve contexts.
    A5 match-day rules and responsibilities Mandatory in substance, with competition-specific tables. Extract competition-specific minimum medical staff table into a separate research note if needed.
    B1 medical note keeping and injury audit Mandatory. Confirm required record content, retention, system expectations, and audit reporting.
    B4 serious injury/death reporting Mandatory. Align with club incident, board, insurer, safeguarding, and possible CQC notification analysis.
    B5 medical emergency action plan Mandatory. Confirm evidence needed for each venue and temporary venue change.
    C1 mandatory medical equipment and drugs box Mandatory. Confirm home/away responsibilities and evidence of pre-match checks.
    C2 equipment list Mixed mandatory and best practice. Separate minimum mandatory equipment from best-practice equipment.
    D anti-doping Mandatory elements and information-only special topics. Separate checking obligations from guidance and education material.
    E concussion Mandatory regulations. Confirm reporting deadlines, professional-game scope, community-game boundary, and loan-player notification.
    F cardiac screening Mandatory for specified competitions including Championship. Confirm provider, registration/exemption, abnormal result, and clearance evidence.
    F mental health and other policies Mixed mandatory, best-practice, and information-only content. Classify each subsection and identify which controls are medical, welfare, safeguarding, or HR-led.

    How to read the map¶

    Field Meaning
    RFL area Clause or clause group in the 2026 standards.
    Evidence to hold Practical records a club should retain or generate.
    Primary owner Usual accountable role.
    Cadence When the evidence should be checked.
    CQC lens Closest CQC-style assurance domain.
    Platform implication What an online platform should capture or automate.

    Section A: Medical personnel¶

    RFL area Evidence to hold Primary owner Cadence CQC lens Platform implication
    A1: Medical staff registration with the RFL RFL registration status, professional registration, role, scope, indemnity, DBS where relevant, expiry dates. Head of Medical Pre-season and before appointment Safe; well-led Workforce register with expiry alerts and evidence uploads.
    A2: Definition of medical staff terms Role descriptions for doctor, physiotherapist, paramedic, sports rehabilitator, sports therapist, first aider, and locum/second clinician. Head of Medical Pre-season and onboarding Safe; effective Role templates mapped to permitted duties and escalation limits.
    A3: Prehospital trauma courses IMMOFP/iIMMOFP or accepted equivalent, course dates, recertification, evidence of practical competence. Head of Medical Pre-season and expiry Safe; effective Credential matrix with blocked deployment if mandatory training lapses.
    A4: Medical staff attendance at matches and training Match and training cover rota, acceptance of fixture cover, absences, substitutions, locum induction. Head of Medical and operations Every fixture and training block Safe; responsive Cover planner linked to fixture and training calendars.
    A5a-f: Match-day medical roles and responsibilities Named medical team, home/away responsibilities, communications equipment checks, pitch-side treatment rules, second clinician arrangements, competition-specific minimum cover. Match-day doctor or lead clinician Every fixture Safe; well-led Match-day checklist with role assignment, confirmations, and exception log.
    A6: Urgent medical cover situations Contingency plan for loss of doctor, paramedic, ambulance, equipment, communications, or venue access. Head of Medical and match operations Every fixture and incident review Safe; responsive Contingency workflow with "cannot proceed" decision points.
    A7: Medical duties Accountability for mandatory equipment, venue readiness, medical decisions, documentation, and player welfare. Match-day lead clinician Every fixture Safe; effective; caring Duty checklist and after-action confirmation.
    A8: Medical treatment for teams travelling to France Travel medical plan, insurance, records, medicine controls, French provider arrangements, legal/indemnity review. Head of Medical and operations Before relevant travel Safe; well-led Travel service-risk checklist and document pack.

    Section B: Ethics, governance, planning, information and data¶

    RFL area Evidence to hold Primary owner Cadence CQC lens Platform implication
    B1: Medical note keeping, RFL research, injury audit Clinical record policy, named record system, record completeness audit, injury audit submissions, research/data-sharing approvals. Head of Medical Continuous; quarterly audit Safe; effective; well-led Secure records/evidence index with completeness dashboard.
    B2: Sharing information Consent/confidentiality policy, player information sheet, disclosure log, role-based access model. Head of Medical and data protection lead Pre-season and each disclosure Caring; well-led Information-sharing workflow with reason, recipient, consent/legal basis, and audit trail.
    B3: Loan-player information sharing Loan medical handover record, parent-club notification, injury/concussion/medicine status, access limits. Head of Medical Each loan movement and incident Safe; responsive Loan-player workflow with mandatory handover fields.
    B4: Reporting death or serious injury Serious incident protocol, notification record, incident chronology, board notification, learning review. Head of Medical and chief executive Each incident Safe; well-led Serious incident case file and notification checklist.
    B5: Medical emergency action plan Site-specific EAP for stadium and training venues, ambulance access, emergency department details, communications route, next-of-kin route, review date. Head of Medical and operations Pre-season, venue change, match change Safe; responsive Venue EAP module with version control and sharing log.
    B6: Pre-match briefing by home club Briefing attendance, agenda, emergency roles, equipment confirmation, ambulance access, opposition sign-off. Home club lead clinician Every home fixture Safe; well-led Digital pre-match briefing form with opposition acknowledgement.
    B7a-e: On-field processes Green card, blood bin, kick-off, 18th player, injured-player process records and post-match exception review. Match-day lead clinician Every fixture Safe; effective Fixture process checklist and exception reporting.

    Section C: Equipment and facilities¶

    RFL area Evidence to hold Primary owner Cadence CQC lens Platform implication
    C1: Mandatory medical equipment and mandatory drugs box Equipment inventory, drug box list, expiry dates, oxygen/AED checks, missing item escalation. Head of Medical Before fixtures/training and monthly Safe Equipment register with barcode/photo evidence and expiry alerts.
    C2: Full minimum equipment and best practice list Competition-specific equipment checklist, best-practice gap log, action plan. Head of Medical Pre-season and quarterly Safe; effective Checklist library by competition and venue type.
    C3: Clinical waste disposal Waste contract, sharps process, collection records, spill process, incident log. Head of Medical and operations Monthly and after incidents Safe Waste compliance record and incident linkage.
    C4: Treatment-room facility standards Room audit, stretcher access, handwashing, cleanable surfaces, privacy, internet access for notes, clinical waste, infection prevention. Head of Medical and venue operations Pre-season, venue change, quarterly Safe; caring Venue facility audit with photo evidence and action tracking.

    Section D: Anti-doping¶

    RFL area Evidence to hold Primary owner Cadence CQC lens Platform implication
    D1: Anti-doping Anti-doping policy, education records, player/support-person acknowledgements, UKAD/RFL communication records. Head of Medical and anti-doping lead Pre-season and updates Safe; well-led Policy acknowledgement and training register.
    D2: Checking medication and supplements Medication check record, Global DRO / UKAD Search Check Apply evidence, supplement risk discussion, prescribing notes. Prescriber or lead clinician Each medicine/supplement decision Safe; effective Medicine check workflow linked to player and clinician.
    D3: Prohibited substances and TUE TUE decision record, proactive/retroactive TUE status, supporting clinical evidence, submission and outcome dates. Club doctor Each relevant treatment Safe; well-led TUE tracker with deadline and document controls.
    D3a-c: IV infusions and TUE routes IV indication, volume, setting, clinician, TUE assessment, emergency rationale if applicable. Club doctor Each IV use Safe; effective High-risk medicine/procedure form with escalation.
    D4-D5: Special topics and beta-2 agonists Inhaler medicine checks, diagnosis evidence, dose advice, player education, exception review. Club doctor Each relevant prescription/review Safe Condition-specific medicine checklist.

    Section E: Concussion and head injuries¶

    RFL area Evidence to hold Primary owner Cadence CQC lens Platform implication
    E1: Purpose, scope and interpretation Club concussion policy, RFL guidance acknowledgement, player/staff education. Head of Medical Pre-season and rule change Safe; well-led Policy versioning and training acknowledgement.
    E2: Pre-season baselines and assessments Baseline register, Cognigram status, SCAT6 status, unavailable/invalid baseline plan. Head of Medical Before contact training and season Safe; effective Baseline dashboard with "not cleared for contact" status.
    E2.1-E2.5: Cognigram, SCAT6 and neurological assessments Test reports, invalid baseline exception, additional assessment rationale. Club doctor or delegated clinician Pre-season and post-injury Safe; effective Assessment record store linked to clearance workflow.
    E3: Recognise and remove Removal record, signs/symptoms, pitch-side decision, player refusal/escalation if relevant. Match/training clinician Each suspected head injury Safe; caring Incident form with mandatory "removed/not returned" fields.
    E3.2: Pitch-side replay Availability and use of replay, limitation where unavailable, decision note. Match-day doctor Each relevant fixture Safe Replay availability field in fixture setup.
    E4: HIA process HIA form, time removed, assessment outcome, structural head injury screen, interchange notifications. Match-day doctor Each HIA Safe; effective HIA workflow with mandatory timestamps and upload/export.
    E5: Post-match and training 3-48 hour aftercare advice, serial evaluations, welfare checks, transport/driving advice. Club doctor or delegated clinician Each concussion/suspected concussion Caring; responsive Follow-up schedule and player advice confirmation.
    E6: Graduated return to play GRTP stage record, symptom checks, doctor oversight, failed-stage action, final clearance. Club doctor Each concussion Safe; effective GRTP workflow with stage gates and clearance lock.
    E7: Doctor concussion reporting RFL concussion report, 24-hour submission evidence, parent-club notification, return-to-play form, post-injury Cognigram, specialist reports. Club doctor Each concussion Safe; well-led Deadline tracker and export pack for RFL/club board.
    E8: SCOAT SCOAT use, findings, follow-up plan, specialist referral where required. Club doctor Each relevant office assessment Effective Assessment upload and referral tracker.

    F1-F2: Blood-borne infectious diseases BBV policy, bleeding injury process, hepatitis B screening/vaccination status where relevant, contaminated equipment process. Head of Medical Pre-season and incidents Safe BBV checklist linked to infection-control and player records.
    F3: Cardiac screening Screening plan, provider contract, provider registration/exemption rationale, attendance, abnormal result pathway, clearance and communication records. Head of Medical and club doctor Annually where mandatory Safe; effective; well-led Screening workflow with provider due diligence and abnormal-result action tracking.
    F3.1-F3.4: Cardiac screening general, requirements, frequency, providers Questionnaire, physical exam, ECG/echo or provider-defined results as applicable, provider credentials, annual completion status. Club doctor Annually and on player onboarding Safe; effective Clinical screening register and participation gate.
    F4: Turning players over on field of play Staff training, spinal injury protocol, scenario practice, incident review. Head of Medical Pre-season and after incident Safe Training record and simulation log.
    F5: Protective and other equipment Advice records on head guards, mouth guards, boxes, padding, player acknowledgement. Clinician or welfare lead Pre-season and as needed Safe; caring Player education and advice log.
    F6-F8: Mental health counselling, mental health training, insomnia Counselling route, crisis pathway, mental health training record, insomnia advice/escalation pathway. Head of Medical and welfare lead Pre-season and case-by-case Caring; responsive Welfare referral directory and training dashboard.
    F9: Social and non-prescribed prescription drugs policy Policy acknowledgement, concern record, case review, RFL escalation where required, confidential support route. Head of Medical and welfare lead Pre-season and each case Safe; caring; well-led Protected case-management workflow with restricted access.
    F10: Loan protocols Loan handover, fitness status, injury/concussion/medicine disclosure controls, parent-club updates. Head of Medical Each loan movement Safe; responsive Loan-player transfer checklist.
    F11: Infection control policy Infection control policy, audit, cleaning records, outbreak/escalation process, PPE and hand hygiene evidence. Head of Medical and operations Quarterly and incidents Safe Infection-control audit and action tracker.

    Evidence pack generated from the clause map¶

    An online platform should be able to generate these packs from the above records:

    • Board assurance pack: compliance dashboard, overdue risks, incidents, action plan, and service-model changes.
    • RFL assurance pack: medical staff, equipment, EAP, concussion, cardiac screening, anti-doping, and mandatory policy evidence.
    • CQC scope pack: service description, provider/control model, sports exclusion rationale, diagnostics/clinic/remote advice checks, and evidence that governance exists even where registration is not required.
    • Incident pack: serious injury/death chronology, notifications, clinical records, debrief, learning, duty-of-candour analysis where relevant.
    • Player transfer/loan pack: agreed medical handover, confidentiality basis, current restrictions, concussion status, and medicines/TUE status.

    Gaps to resolve before solution design¶

    • Confirm competition-specific variations for Championship, Super League, Women's Super League, academy, reserves, and community contexts.
    • Confirm which RFL evidence is mandatory, best practice, or information only for the specific club.
    • Decide whether the platform stores clinical notes directly or references a specialist sports EMR.
    • Define board-visible summaries that avoid inappropriate disclosure of confidential player health data.
    • Define CQC/legal review points for diagnostics, screening, prescribing, remote advice, ambulance transport, and public/private clinics.
    • Validate the map with someone who has operational responsibility for RFL medical compliance.
    • Link each evidence type to the Minimum Compliance Pack Research inventory before converting it into templates or software fields.
