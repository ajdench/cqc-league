    Scope Decision Record¶

    This page records the current scope decision for the research. It should be revisited when the intended output changes.

    Decision¶

    The working scope is an online research and preparation platform for a Championship Rugby League team, using a Championship club as the starting example.

    The platform should help the organisation:

    • appreciate what CQC is and why it may matter;
    • understand how a CQC-style assessment or inspection would work;
    • identify whether its medical service is outside scope, excluded, contractor-led, or potentially registerable;
    • understand what evidence it would need to prepare;
    • move from existing medical/clinical governance to CQC inspection-level readiness;
    • do this in a context where CQC preparation is new to the organisation.

    This does not mean the club is assumed to require CQC registration. The research remains focused on understanding, preparation, and evidence.

    Audience¶

    The audience is not yet a commercial buying group. The primary audience is a Championship Rugby League organisation that needs to learn and prepare.

    Audience Need
    Board/CEO Understand risk, accountability, inspection exposure, decisions needed, and what assurance should look like.
    Head of Medical Translate existing clinical governance into CQC-style evidence, gaps, and preparation actions.
    Clinical team Understand records, professional boundaries, incidents, medicines, concussion, confidentiality, and evidence expectations.
    Operations/match-day staff Understand EAP, venue, treatment-room, equipment, contractor, ambulance, and communication evidence.
    Safeguarding/welfare staff Understand where clinical governance overlaps with welfare, academy, mental health, and restricted information.
    External providers Understand what evidence the club needs from cardiac screening, event medical, diagnostics, or other contractors.

    Immediate output¶

    The immediate output is an online preparation and assurance knowledge base, not a software implementation specification.

    It should be able to support:

    • a board-facing Head of Medical briefing;
    • a CQC scope decision record;
    • a minimum compliance/evidence pack;
    • RFL standards evidence mapping;
    • practical worked examples;
    • a gap analysis against inspection-style expectations;
    • a preparation route for a club that has never approached CQC before.

    Rejected or secondary scopes¶

    Scope Current treatment
    Full SaaS product specification Deferred. Too early until research and validation are clearer.
    Commercial buyer/persona modelling Deferred. Audience is organisational learning and preparation, not procurement.
    All professional sports Secondary. Rugby League and Championship context should stay concrete first.
    All Rugby League competitions Secondary. Championship is the starting example; Super League, WSL, academy, reserves, and community are comparison points.
    Legal advice product Out of scope. The wiki can identify advice triggers but not provide legal determinations.
    Clinical decision-support tool Out of scope. Clinical safety and medical-device issues would need separate analysis.

    What this means for roadmap items¶

    Item Scope implication
    1. Research audience and decision context Now provisionally decided: online preparation platform for a Championship Rugby League organisation new to CQC.
    2. Minimum compliance pack Should focus on what a club needs to understand and gather before inspection-style scrutiny, not on final product forms.
    3. RFL clause map Should prioritise Championship relevance and board/Head of Medical interpretation.
    4. Platform information architecture Remains deferred, but future work can consider knowledge-base structure and preparation journey before software data models.
    5. CQC scope question bank Should be phrased as guided preparation and fact gathering, not an automated registration answer.
    6. Tool landscape Should remain a comparator for what existing tools can teach or support, not a vendor selection exercise.

    Assumptions to test¶

    • A Championship club may have medical and clinical governance, but not in a form that is obviously ready for CQC-style inspection.
    • A board and Head of Medical need plain-English preparation material before operational tooling.
    • The most useful first output is likely a guided evidence and understanding pathway, not a transactional compliance system.
    • The club needs to separate board assurance from confidential clinical records.
    • Existing tools may support parts of the evidence burden, but the club first needs to know what evidence matters.

    Open decisions¶

    Open decision Why it matters
    Is Sheffield Eagles the named working example, or should the site remain generic Championship? Determines tone, examples, venue specificity, and board-pack realism.
    Should academy/scholarship be included in first-phase scope? Adds safeguarding, parental consent, and child welfare complexity.
    Should the online platform be public education, internal club preparation, or both? Changes language, confidentiality, and evidence examples.
    Should the next output be a guided preparation pathway through the wiki? Would change navigation from research sections to task-based preparation.
    Should the platform compare "current governance" against "CQC inspection-grade governance"? Could be the clearest bridge for a club new to CQC.

    Next research direction¶

    The next useful research direction is to define the preparation journey:

    1. What a Championship club needs to understand first.
    2. What facts it must collect.
    3. What evidence it must hold.
    4. What gaps it should expect.
    5. What should be escalated to legal/CQC/RFL/vendor review.

    That journey should be written for a club that has not previously prepared for CQC and may not know what "inspection-grade" evidence looks like.
