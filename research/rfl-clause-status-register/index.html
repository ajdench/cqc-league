    RFL Clause Status Register¶

    This page hardens item 3 by separating RFL status, Championship relevance, record visibility, and validation gaps. It should be read with RFL Clause Evidence Map.

    The register is based on the RFL Medical Standards 2026. It is a research register, not a substitute for the RFL source document.

    Status definitions¶

    Status Meaning for this research
    Mandatory The RFL standards expressly identify the area as mandatory or mandatory in substance.
    Competition-specific mandatory Mandatory for specified competitions, teams, roles, or circumstances.
    Best practice The RFL standards present the area as best practice or recommended.
    Information only The RFL standards present the area as information or guidance rather than a direct action requirement.
    Mixed The section contains mandatory and non-mandatory elements that need separating before final evidence design.
    Needs validation The source status is not yet sufficiently classified for a club-specific pack.

    Status register¶

    RFL area Research status Championship relevance Club-held evidence Clinician-only or restricted evidence Validation gap
    A1: Medical staff registration Mandatory Relevant to club medical staff and match/training cover. RFL registration status, professional registration, indemnity, role, expiry dates. Professional fitness issues, occupational health details. Confirm role-by-role evidence route and whether contractor staff are evidenced by club or provider.
    A2: Medical staff terms Mixed Relevant because role definitions drive cover and delegation. Role descriptions, scope matrix, cover rota. Individual supervision notes where sensitive. Confirm which roles are allowed for Championship home, away, training, academy, and reserves.
    A3: Prehospital trauma courses Competition-specific mandatory Relevant for field-of-play cover and IMMOFP/iIMMOFP expectations. Course certificates, expiry dates, accepted equivalent record. Individual training remediation. Confirm accepted equivalents and grace periods.
    A4: Medical staff attendance Mandatory Relevant for matches and training. Fixture/training cover plan, locum induction, attendance exceptions. Individual sickness/HR records. Extract exact Championship cover requirements from the RFL competition table.
    A5: Match-day rules and responsibilities Mixed mandatory and best practice Highly relevant to home and away Championship fixtures. Match-day medical plan, pre-match confirmations, communication checks, equipment checks, exception log. Clinical decision notes and player treatment records. Separate A5a-f by competition and home/away duty.
    A6: Urgent medical cover situations Information only Relevant as contingency planning even if stated for information. Contingency plan, postponement/escalation decision record. Clinician-specific availability issues. Confirm what should be board assurance versus match operations procedure.
    A7: Medical duties Mandatory in substance Relevant to lead clinician accountability. Duty checklist, equipment sign-off, post-match confirmation. Individual clinical notes. Clarify whether duties sit with club doctor, lead physiotherapist, or contractor in mixed models.
    A8: Treatment in France Information only Relevant only when travelling to France/Toulouse or similar cross-border fixtures. Travel medical plan, insurance/indemnity check, medicine/travel records. Individual health records. Confirm when this applies to Championship clubs.
    B1: Medical note keeping and injury audit Mandatory Relevant to all club medical care. Record-system policy, access control, record completeness audit, injury audit evidence. Full clinical records. Confirm retention, audit sample, and RFL submission evidence.
    B2: Sharing information Mandatory in substance Relevant to players, coaches, contractors, parent clubs, and league reporting. Consent/confidentiality policy, disclosure log, role-access matrix. Detailed disclosure content and clinical reports. Align with GMC confidentiality and club data protection model.
    B3: Loan-player information sharing Mandatory Relevant where loan players are used. Loan handover checklist, notification record, restriction status. Detailed medical history. Confirm parent-club and loan-club minimum data set.
    B4: Death or serious injury reporting Mandatory Relevant to all matches/training. Serious incident chronology, notifications, board escalation, learning review. Clinical records and identifiable incident detail. Align RFL, insurer, safeguarding, and possible CQC notification routes.
    B5: Medical emergency action plan Mandatory Relevant for stadium, training, temporary venues, and venue moves. Site-specific EAP, ambulance access, ED details, review date, sharing log. Personal contact details where sensitive. Confirm temporary venue evidence standard.
    B6: Pre-match briefing Mandatory for home club Relevant to every home fixture. Briefing record, attendee list, emergency roles, opposition acknowledgement. None usually, unless player-specific risk is discussed. Define standard briefing evidence.
    B7: On-field processes Mixed Relevant where green card, blood bin, kick-off, or 18th-player rules apply. Fixture process checklist and exception log. Individual incident clinical record. Confirm which subsections apply to Championship and which are Super League-only.
    C1: Mandatory medical equipment and drugs box Mandatory Relevant to matches/training and travel. Inventory, expiry checks, oxygen/AED checks, missing-item escalation. Controlled medicine administration records. Confirm home/away and training responsibilities.
    C2: Equipment list Mixed mandatory and best practice Relevant to minimum kit and best-practice enhancements. Minimum mandatory equipment checklist, best-practice gap log. None usually. Separate mandatory kit from best-practice kit for Championship.
    C3: Clinical waste disposal Mandatory Relevant to treatment room and match/training care. Clinical waste contract/process, sharps checks, disposal audit. Incident records if exposure occurs. Confirm venue/contractor split.
    C4: Treatment-room facility standards Mandatory Relevant to training ground and stadium medical room. Room audit, access, handwashing, cleanable surfaces, privacy, internet, waste. None usually. Confirm temporary venue requirements.
    D1-D5: Anti-doping and medication checks Mixed mandatory and information-only Relevant to all player medicines and supplements. Policy, education records, medicine-check audit, TUE tracker status. Indication, diagnosis, prescription details, TUE medical evidence. Separate mandatory checks from special-topic guidance.
    E1-E8: Concussion and head injuries Mandatory regulations, with some competition-specific mechanisms Highly relevant to Championship. Baseline completion dashboard, HIA/RTP compliance status, reporting deadlines, exception actions. HIA form, SCAT6/Cognigram, clinical notes, specialist reports. Confirm Championship pitch-side replay, reporting deadline, and specialist referral requirements.
    F1-F2: Blood-borne infectious diseases Mandatory, with some best-practice elements Relevant to match/training bleeding injuries and infection control. BBV policy, bleeding injury process, hepatitis B screening/vaccination status at aggregate level. Individual screening/vaccination data. Confirm which screening/vaccination requirements apply to Championship.
    F3: Cardiac screening Competition-specific mandatory Relevant because RFL status includes Championship in the cardiac screening section. Screening plan, provider due diligence, completion dashboard, abnormal-result pathway assurance. Individual cardiac reports and referral records. Confirm provider registration/exemption model and exact player cohorts.
    F4: Turning players over Mandatory Relevant to all field-of-play care. Training record, spinal protocol, scenario rehearsal. Incident clinical details. Confirm training cadence.
    F5: Protective and other equipment Information only, with competition-specific equipment rules Relevant where mouth guards, head guards, boxes, padding, or instrumented mouth guards apply. Advice/education records and policy position. Individual medical exemptions. Confirm Championship-specific equipment obligations.
    F6-F8: Mental health counselling, training, insomnia Mixed information-only and mandatory training Relevant to player welfare and staff training. Welfare route, mental health training record, counselling access summary. Individual mental health records and referrals. Separate welfare, medical, HR, and safeguarding ownership.
    F9: Social and non-prescribed prescription drugs Mixed policy requirement and support pathway Relevant to player welfare, anti-doping, safeguarding, and discipline. Policy acknowledgement, case-review route, aggregate assurance. Individual case file. Confirm RFL escalation threshold and access restrictions.
    F10: Loan protocols Best practice Relevant where loan players move between clubs. Loan handover checklist and parent-club communication. Detailed clinical record. Decide whether to treat as local mandatory control despite best-practice RFL status.
    F11: Infection control Needs validation Relevant to treatment room, match-day care, and outbreaks. Infection-control policy, audit, cleaning record, action plan. Individual exposure/infection records. Confirm status wording and relationship to C3/F1-F2.

    Championship-specific research notes¶

    • Cardiac screening is identified in the RFL standards as mandatory for specified competitions including Championship, but provider/control evidence still needs a CQC diagnostic/screening review.
    • Mandatory medical equipment and drugs box requirements apply across club contexts; exact home, away, training, academy, reserves, and scholarship responsibilities need extracting into a club-specific checklist.
    • Championship match-day cover requirements appear in competition tables; these should be converted into a short Championship cover register before any implementation pack is finalised.
    • Some controls are RFL best practice but may still be locally necessary because they reduce CQC-style governance risk.
