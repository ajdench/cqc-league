    Source Map¶

    This page records the main sources used and the point each source supports.

    Source Used for
    CQC: Scope of registration Registration trigger, offence risk, decision questions, latest guidance caveat.
    CQC: The regulated activities Need to register for each relevant regulated activity; no hierarchy of activities.
    CQC: Treatment of disease, disorder or injury Current 2026 sports exclusions, first-aid exclusions, medicines administration, supervision, and professional role scope. The sports-ground/event wording is not limited to amateur sport.
    CQC: Diagnostic and screening procedures Imaging, ultrasound, sample analysis, physiological measurement, remote reporting, and sports medicine diagnostics.
    CQC: Transport services, triage and medical advice provided remotely Ambulance/event-site transport boundary and urgent remote advice/triage registration trigger.
    CQC: Types of service provider or legal entity Hosting, subcontracting, joint ventures, and control/accountability tests.
    CQC: Register as a provider Application readiness, supporting documents, registered managers, premises, controlled drugs, and offence warning.
    CQC: Statement of purpose Statement of purpose content and update expectations.
    CQC: Scope of Registration Guidance May 2022 PDF Treatment of disease, disorder or injury and the sports-ground, gymnasium, first-aid, and temporary sporting-event exclusions.
    CQC: What is registration? Provider legal entity concepts and registration basics.
    CQC: What is a location? Provider identity, subcontracting, responsibility, and location analysis.
    CQC: Applying as a new provider guidance Nominated individual, registered manager, location details, and need for systems before registration.
    CQC: Regulation 12, Safe care and treatment Risk assessment, competence, medicines, infection prevention, emergency planning.
    CQC: Regulation 15, Premises and equipment Clean, suitable, maintained premises and equipment.
    CQC: Regulation 16, Complaints Accessible complaint system and CQC information requests.
    CQC: Regulation 17, Good governance Governance systems, audit, risk, information, records, staff and management records.
    CQC: Regulation 18, Staffing Sufficient qualified, competent, skilled, experienced staff and support.
    CQC: Regulation 19, Fit and proper persons employed Recruitment checks and fitness for clinical roles.
    CQC: Regulation 20, Duty of candour Openness and notifiable safety incident duties for regulated providers.
    CQC: Assessment framework Safe, effective, caring, responsive, and well-led assessment lens.
    CQC: Assessment Current provider assessment approach and links to assessing quality, framework, and evidence categories.
    CQC: Levels of ratings Ratings levels for independent doctors, clinics, single specialty services, hospitals, and other services.
    CQC: Factual accuracy portal Draft report factual accuracy process, portal access, evidence uploads, and submission handling.
    CQC: Appendix 1, the 34 quality statements Provider quality statement names and mapping across the five key questions.
    CQC: Evidence categories Six evidence categories used to assess quality statements.
    CQC: Governance, management and sustainability Well-led quality statement and evidence themes.
    CQC: Medicines optimisation Medicines safety, best practice, prescribing/supply/administration, transfer information, and controlled-drugs oversight.
    CQC: Enforcement action and representations Provider representations against enforcement action and appeal route for notices of decision.
    CQC: Warning Notices Warning Notice triggers, representations, compliance follow-up, publication, and escalation risk.
    CQC: Independent healthcare services Definition and examples of independent healthcare providers.
    CQC: Local authority assessment framework Distinction between provider assessment and local authority assurance using four themes and nine quality statements.
    RFL: Medical Management IMMOFP/iIMMOFP roles, professional registration, indemnity, and training requirements.
    RFL: Medical Standards 2026 PDF Current RFL mandatory medical personnel, records, information sharing, EAP, equipment, anti-doping, concussion, cardiac screening, and medical policy controls.
    RFL: Concussion Statement Professional game medical provision, concussion governance, CPD, community first-aider expectations.
    RFL: Anti-Doping Rugby League anti-doping rules, TUEs, testing, and player/support-person obligations.
    UKAD: Medicine and TUE Hub TUE concept and medicine checking route.
    UKAD: Search Check Apply Medication checking, Global DRO, TUE Wizard, and athlete responsibility.
    UKAD: Managing Supplement Risks Strict liability and supplement contamination risk.
    GMC: Good practice in prescribing and managing medicines and devices Prescribing competence, safe assessment, records, controlled-drugs safeguards, follow-up, and adverse reaction reporting.
    GMC: Confidentiality, employment and similar purposes Sports-team dual obligations, consent, relevance, reports, and disclosure boundaries.
    Fieldfisher: Sports organisations and CQC registration Secondary analysis on sports organisations, registration rarity, and governance burden.
    Sheffield Eagles: Stadium move statement, 23 April 2026 Sheffield Eagles Championship operating context and venue-change example for emergency action planning.
    LLM Wiki Reference pattern for a Material for MkDocs-based LLM-facing wiki.
    mkdocs-llms-source on PyPI LLM outputs: /llms.txt, /llms-full.txt, and per-page Markdown copies.
    Material for MkDocs Documentation theme and site structure.
    GOV.UK: Writing for GOV.UK Page-length principle: no universal minimum or maximum; length depends on user need and clarity.
    GOV.UK: Planning content Avoid broad "everything you need to know" pages that are hard to navigate.
    GOV.UK Design System: Pagination Pattern for splitting related content across multiple pages when a single page becomes hard to use.
    Newcastle University: Readability and writing for the web Web users scan content; supports short, clearly structured pages with headings and chunks.
    Radar Healthcare: CQC Compliance Software Example of CQC-focused compliance/evidence, audit, action, and inspection readiness tooling.
    Radar Healthcare: Audit Management Software Example of healthcare audit workflow, action tracking, and evidence capture.
    Vantage: Compliance and Quality Management Example of CQC compliance, quality management, governance, audit, and evidence tooling for health and care providers.
    QCS Example of policy, procedure, audit, and compliance support for regulated health and care providers.
    Kitman Labs: Performance Medicine Example of elite sports medicine records, injury/illness management, and athlete health workflow.
    Teamworks AMS / Smartabase Example of athlete management, sports science, and medical data platform used in performance environments.
    AthleteMonitoring: Electronic Medical Records Example of sports EMR, injury tracking, SOAP notes, medication, and return-to-play record capability.
    SportsWareOnLine Example of athlete health record, injury, treatment, rehabilitation, and reporting software.
    Orreco: Medical Notes Example of sports medical notes and athlete health data capture.
    SportLoMo: Governance and Safe Sport Example of sports governance, safe sport, certification, and participant-management tooling.
    Globocol: Health and Safety in Sport Software Example of sports health and safety, incident, risk, and compliance management tooling.
    CPOMS StudentSafe for Sports Clubs Example of safeguarding and welfare case-management tooling adapted to sports clubs.
    Sport:80 Compliance Management Example of sports compliance, eligibility, certifications, and document/workflow management.

    NHS Digital: Clinical safety developer guidance Practical clinical safety guidance for health IT developers.
