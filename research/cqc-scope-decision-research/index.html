    CQC Scope Decision Research¶

    This page records the research needed before any "CQC scope decision wizard" is designed. The goal is to understand the regulatory decision, not to jump directly to software.

    The core research question is: what facts must a professional Rugby League club establish before it can safely conclude that a medical service is outside scope, excluded, contractor-led, or potentially registerable?

    Use this page with Registration Decision Model, CQC Registration Edge Cases, Minimum Compliance Pack Research, and Worked Examples.

    Source anchors¶

    CQC's scope guidance identifies the main questions: whether a regulated activity is carried on, who directs and controls it, whether exceptions apply, where it is carried on, and whether registered manager or other registration requirements follow.

    For Rugby League, the most important regulated activity is usually treatment of disease, disorder or injury. CQC's current guidance says that this activity can include emergency treatment and treatment by or under supervision of specified healthcare professionals, but it excludes treatment in a sports ground or gymnasium where provided solely for people taking part in or attending sporting activities and events, and temporary arrangements for sporting or cultural events.

    Other activities may still matter, especially diagnostic and screening procedures and transport services, triage and medical advice provided remotely.

    Decision facts to research¶

    Decision area Research question Evidence to collect Why it matters
    Service description What exactly is being provided? Service description, location list, users, clinician roles, activities, payment route, records, referrals, follow-up. CQC registration depends on activity and control, not the label "club medical".
    User group Is care solely for players/participants/attendees, or is it offered to staff, public, sponsors, schools, or private patients? Eligibility rules, booking forms, communications, invoices, website copy. Public or wider private care may undermine reliance on sports exclusions.
    Activity Is there treatment of disease, disorder or injury, diagnostics, screening, remote advice, triage, transport, surgery, prescribing, injections, IV treatment, mental health care, or rehabilitation? Clinical pathway, SOPs, medicine lists, diagnostic/screening forms, remote advice logs. Different regulated activities have different tests and exclusions.
    Professional involvement Is care by, or under supervision of, a listed healthcare professional for the relevant regulated activity? Role register, professional registration, supervision protocol, delegated task record. CQC's treatment activity turns on specified professional involvement or supervision.
    Sports exclusion Is treatment at a sports ground, gymnasium, associated premises, or temporary event, and solely for those taking part in or attending the event? Venue map, event plan, fixture/training schedule, access rules, attendee/participant evidence. This is the central Rugby League exclusion question.
    Provider/control Who directs and controls clinical policy, staff, records, medicines, complaints, incidents, quality, and data decisions? Contracts, governance chart, policies, record-system ownership, incident routes, complaint process. A club can create exposure if it controls a registerable activity even where clinicians are contractors.
    Location Where is the activity carried on at or from? Stadium/training ground details, third-party premises, remote base, mobile unit, away/travel plan. CQC location rules can matter if registration is required.
    Contractor status If an external provider is used, is it registered, exempt, or out of scope for the actual service? CQC provider profile, regulated activities, location conditions, written exemption analysis, contract. Contractor registration does not automatically cover everything the club does.
    Change triggers What would invalidate the previous conclusion? Change log, new service proposal, new user group, new premises, new medicine/procedure, new contractor. A scope conclusion must be reviewed when facts change.

    Provisional outcome types¶

    These are research labels, not legal conclusions.

    Outcome label Meaning Minimum evidence
    Outside CQC scope The service does not appear to involve a CQC regulated activity. Service description, activity analysis, professional involvement analysis, review date.
    Sports/event exclusion likely applies Treatment appears limited to sports-ground/gym/associated premises or temporary event provision, solely for participants or attendees. Venue/event evidence, user-group rules, activity description, exclusion rationale.
    Contractor-led An external provider appears to carry on the activity and the club is commissioning/hosting rather than directing clinical provision. Contract, provider registration/exemption evidence, control analysis, quality reporting route.
    Mixed or borderline Facts are unclear or activities/user groups are mixed. Gap list, risk analysis, pause/ring-fence recommendation, legal/CQC advice record.
    Potential registration risk Club may carry on a regulated activity without a clear exclusion. Regulated activity analysis, provider/control evidence, locations, registered manager/nominated individual considerations, advice plan.

    Research tasks for item 5¶

    Task Output
    Convert each edge case into decision facts Update CQC Registration Edge Cases with the factual questions that drive the answer.
    Build a question bank Create neutral research questions before building any interface.
    Define evidence required per answer Link each answer to documents, records, screenshots, contracts, or board approvals.
    Identify "stop and seek advice" points Mark public clinics, diagnostics, remote advice, IV services, prescribing expansion, ambulance transport, and shared-control models.
    Test against worked examples Check the question set against player-only care, concussion, cardiac screening, academy, public clinic, contractor, and remote triage scenarios.
    Validate language Ensure the tool says "appears", "likely", and "seek advice" where appropriate, not "CQC compliant".

    Research-complete definition¶

    Item 5 should only be marked research-complete when:

    • the decision facts above have been tested against the Worked Examples;
    • each possible outcome has minimum supporting evidence;
    • unclear or high-risk fact patterns trigger advice rather than an automated conclusion;
    • contractor and hosted-service questions are explicit;
    • change triggers are documented so the conclusion is not treated as permanent.
