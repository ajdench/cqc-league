    Assurance Pack Templates¶

    These templates turn the research into evidence a club can use repeatedly. They are intentionally concise so they can be copied into board papers, committee packs, contracts, or internal forms. Use them alongside Minimum Compliance Pack Research, CQC's scope of registration guidance, the RFL Medical Standards 2026, and the detailed decision logic in Registration Decision Model.

    1. CQC scope assessment¶

    Field Entry
    Service name
    Legal entity proposing service
    Service owner
    Clinical governance lead
    Locations Stadium; training ground; academy site; mobile; remote; third-party premises.
    User group Contracted players; academy; staff; officials; public; sponsors; schools; other.
    Activities Treatment; diagnostics; screening; prescribing; injections; remote triage; transport; rehabilitation; mental health; other.
    Regulated activity candidates Treatment of disease, disorder or injury; diagnostic and screening; transport/triage/remote advice; surgical procedures; other.
    Sports/event exclusion analysis Is care solely for participants or attendees at sports ground, gym, associated premises, or temporary event? Evidence?
    Provider/control analysis Who controls clinical policy, staff, records, complaints, incidents, medicines, equipment, referrals, and quality assurance?
    Contractor/host analysis Registered provider? Exemption rationale? Contract responsibility? Audit rights?
    Location analysis Where is the activity delivered, organised, or managed from?
    Registered manager needed?
    Controlled drugs licence issue?
    Conclusion Outside scope; excluded; contractor registered; club must register; legal advice needed.
    Reviewer and date
    Next review trigger New users; public access; diagnostics; prescribing; injections; remote advice; new premises; new contractor.

    This template is intended for the edge cases identified in CQC Registration Edge Cases, especially public clinics, diagnostics, remote triage, prescribing, injections, IV services, and contractor-controlled care.

    For platform design, this template should become a structured workflow rather than a static document. See Platform Product Blueprint.

    2. Medical service statement of purpose¶

    Section Minimum content
    Service purpose What the club medical service exists to do.
    Legal entity Club company/charity/partnership and address.
    Aims and objectives Player welfare, safe participation, emergency care, rehabilitation, compliance with RFL standards.
    People served First team, reserves, academy, women's teams, scholarship, match officials, staff, public exclusions.
    Services provided Match-day care, training care, injury assessment, rehabilitation, concussion management, emergency care, referrals, screening.
    Services not provided Public clinic, paid private treatment, remote urgent triage, IV wellness, diagnostics, or other exclusions unless approved.
    Locations Stadium, training ground, academy, away fixtures, temporary events, remote administration.
    Clinical governance Board committee, accountable executive, clinical governance lead, head doctor, safeguarding lead, information governance lead.
    Records and confidentiality System, access, retention, player consent, coach information boundaries.
    Incidents and complaints How concerns, complaints, serious incidents, safeguarding, and duty-of-candour reviews are handled.
    Contractors External providers, registration/exemption evidence, audit and reporting.
    Review Annual board approval and update after service change.

    This mirrors CQC's statement of purpose discipline even where the club concludes it is outside registration.

    3. Board medical assurance report¶

    Heading Report content
    Executive summary Top three risks, decisions needed, new service changes.
    CQC scope status Current registration/exclusion assessment and any changes.
    RFL compliance Medical staff, IMMOFP, EAP, equipment, concussion, cardiac screening, anti-doping.
    Safe Incidents, emergency transfers, safeguarding, equipment, medicines, staffing gaps.
    Effective Audit results, referral timeliness, return-to-play outcomes, reinjury, records.
    Caring Player feedback, consent, confidentiality, chaperones, wellbeing, pressure concerns.
    Responsive Complaints, access delays, academy/family communication, equality issues.
    Well-led Risk register, overdue actions, contractor assurance, audit closure, speaking-up cases.
    Decisions requested Funding, staffing, facilities, contractor changes, legal/CQC advice, policy approval.

    The report headings follow CQC Assurance Domains and Quality Statements and can be populated from the RFL Medical Standards Map.

    4. Medical risk register fields¶

    Field Example
    Risk ID MED-001
    Risk title Concussion return-to-play breach
    Description Player returns before RFL GRTP and doctor sign-off complete.
    CQC domain Safe; Effective; Well-led.
    RFL standard link Section E concussion and GRTP.
    Cause Fixture pressure, missing baseline, poor loan-player handover.
    Impact Player harm, RFL sanction, regulatory exposure, litigation.
    Controls Baseline register, HIA report, RTP form, specialist review, board dashboard.
    Control owner Head doctor.
    Assurance Monthly audit; exception reporting to board.
    Rating Inherent and residual likelihood/impact.
    Actions Named action, due date, status, evidence of closure.

    6. Contractor due diligence checklist¶

    Check Evidence
    Legal entity and trading name Company/charity/sole trader details.
    Service description What they provide and to whom.
    CQC registration status Provider ID, regulated activities, locations, conditions, or written exemption analysis.
    Clinical governance Policies, incident reporting, complaints, audit, safeguarding, duty of candour.
    Clinician credentials Professional registration, qualifications, indemnity, DBS where relevant, CPD.
    Medicines/equipment Ownership, storage, expiry, controlled-drugs position, maintenance.
    Records System, controller/processor status, access, retention, data sharing, subject access.
    Anti-doping Global DRO/TUE process, supplement controls, athlete education.
    Reporting Monthly/quarterly quality reports, serious incident escalation, RFL reporting.
    Audit rights Club can inspect records/evidence relevant to contract quality and safety.
    Conflict escalation Clinical judgement prevails over sporting or commercial pressure.

    This checklist should be used before relying on an external provider's registration or exemption analysis. See Private Healthcare Synergies for the contracting rationale.

    7. Medicines and anti-doping log¶

    Field Entry
    Player
    Medicine/treatment
    Indication
    Prescriber/administering clinician
    Dose/route/frequency
    Date/time
    Allergy/contraindication check
    Consent recorded
    Global DRO search reference
    In-competition/out-of-competition status
    TUE required? No; prospective; retroactive; pending.
    Batch/expiry where relevant
    Advice and safety-netting
    Follow-up/review

    Use this alongside Medicines, Prescribing, and Anti-Doping, UKAD Search Check Apply, and GMC prescribing guidance.

    8. Concussion assurance checklist¶

    Control Evidence
    Valid annual SCAT6 and Cognigram before contact training Baseline register.
    Recognise-and-remove applied Match/training incident record.
    HIA/concussion report completed within required timescale RFL submission evidence.
    Parent club notified for loan/away scenarios Handover record.
    Player given post-injury advice Information sheet and record entry.
    Medication masking review completed Clinical note.
    GRTP doctor oversight GRTP log.
    Post-injury Cognigram completed before required stage Report on file.
    Specialist review obtained where required Specialist report and player/parent acknowledgment.
    RTP form submitted before match participation Submission evidence.

    9. Annual medical governance review¶

    Agenda:

    1. Current CQC registration/exclusion assessment.
    2. RFL Medical Standards compliance.
    3. Clinical governance structure and named leads.
    4. Incidents, complaints, safeguarding, and serious injury reports.
    5. Concussion and return-to-play audit.
    6. Medicines, prescribing, drugs box, anti-doping, and TUE audit.
    7. Cardiac screening and diagnostics provider assurance.
    8. Records, confidentiality, data sharing, and loan-player handovers.
    9. Contractor due diligence and quality reports.
    10. Player experience and clinician speaking-up feedback.
    11. Facilities, equipment, emergency action plans, and infection control.
    12. Risk register and action plan for next season.
