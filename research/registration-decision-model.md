# Registration Decision Model

Use this model before launching, changing, contracting, or commercialising any club medical service. It operationalises CQC's [scope of registration](https://www.cqc.org.uk/guidance-regulation/providers/registration/scope-registration) and should be read with [CQC Governance Baseline](cqc-governance-baseline.md).

For difficult fact patterns, use this page together with [CQC Registration Edge Cases](cqc-registration-edge-cases.md).

## Step 1: Describe the service

Record:

- service name and owner;
- patient/user group;
- premises and mobile/event locations;
- clinicians and professional roles;
- activities performed;
- medicines, diagnostics, procedures, records, referrals, and follow-up;
- whether anyone pays for the service directly or indirectly;
- whether the service is solely for players, participants, or event attendees.

## Step 2: Identify possible regulated activities

Check whether the service includes:

- [treatment of disease, disorder, or injury](https://www.cqc.org.uk/guidance-regulation/providers/registration/scope-registration/regulated-activities/treatment-disease-disorder-or-injury);
- [diagnostic and screening procedures](https://www.cqc.org.uk/guidance-regulation/providers/registration/scope-registration/regulated-activities/diagnostic-and-screening-procedures);
- surgical procedures;
- [transport, triage, or medical advice provided remotely](https://www.cqc.org.uk/guidance-regulation/providers/registration/scope-registration/regulated-activities/regulated-activities/transport-services-triage-and-medical-advice-provided);
- maternity or midwifery services;
- nursing care or personal care in its own right.

Most Rugby League medical services will start with treatment of disease, disorder, or injury, but diagnostics and screening are common expansion points.

## Step 3: Test exclusions and exemptions

For sport, document whether the activity is:

- first aid in an unexpected or dangerous situation requiring immediate action;
- first aid by trained non-healthcare professionals or a first-aid organisation;
- treatment at a sports ground, gymnasium, or associated premises;
- solely for people taking part in or attending sporting activities and events;
- temporary provision for a sporting event.

If all relevant conditions are met, record the rationale and evidence. If the service also treats the public or provides wider clinic services, do not assume the sports exclusion applies.

## Step 4: Identify who carries on the activity

Ask who controls:

- clinical policy;
- clinician appointment and supervision;
- patient eligibility;
- records system;
- medicines and equipment;
- complaints and incidents;
- data controller decisions;
- referral pathways;
- payment and service terms.

If the club controls these features, the club may be the provider for CQC purposes. If a contractor controls them, check the contractor's registration or exemption. If control is shared, clarify it before service launch using CQC's [legal entity/provider guidance](https://www.cqc.org.uk/guidance-regulation/providers/registration/scope-registration/who-has-register/types-service-provider-or-legal-entity) and the [contractor due diligence checklist](assurance-pack-templates.md#6-contractor-due-diligence-checklist).

## Step 5: Determine locations

Map whether the activity is carried on:

- at a stadium medical room;
- at a training ground;
- at an academy site;
- from a mobile unit;
- remotely from an administrative base;
- from a third-party clinic;
- at away fixtures or temporary events.

CQC [location rules](https://www.cqc.org.uk/guidance-providers/registration/what-location) and location conditions can matter even where the legal entity is clear.

## Step 6: Decide the governance route

| Outcome | Action |
| --- | --- |
| Clearly outside CQC scope | Keep documented rationale and maintain sport/clinical governance. |
| Excluded because sports-ground/event conditions apply | Keep evidence that the service is solely for participants/attendees and not a public clinic. |
| Contractor is responsible and registered | Verify registration, contract clinical governance, and receive quality reports. |
| Contractor is responsible and says exempt | Require written exemption rationale and review annually. |
| Club likely carries on regulated activity | Obtain legal/CQC advice, identify activities, locations, nominated individual, registered manager, and evidence pack. |
| Borderline or mixed model | Pause launch or ring-fence service until control, users, locations, and activities are clear. |

## Minimum documentation pack

- regulated activity analysis;
- sports exclusion analysis;
- service statement of purpose;
- clinical governance structure;
- policies and SOPs;
- contracts and service-level agreements;
- clinician credential register;
- premises and equipment register;
- incident, complaint, and safeguarding process;
- records and data protection process;
- audit plan and board reporting template.
