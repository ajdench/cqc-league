# Minimum Compliance Pack Scenario Matrix

This page tests the [Minimum Compliance Pack Research](minimum-compliance-pack-research.md) against six practical club scenarios. It is still research, not a final product design.

The purpose is to identify:

- what evidence appears necessary;
- which CQC/RFL source creates or supports the need;
- who would usually own the evidence;
- what the board can see without accessing confidential clinical detail;
- what remains uncertain and needs legal, clinical, RFL, or operational validation.

## Summary matrix

| Scenario | Core pack components | Specialist appendices | Research status |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Player-only match/training medical service | Scope decision record; medical service description; RFL dashboard; governance meeting record; board assurance report; annual review. | Clinical records audit; equipment/EAP checks; medicines; concussion; safeguarding. | Baseline evidence need is clear; competition-specific RFL detail still needs validation. |
| External cardiac screening provider | Scope decision record; contractor due diligence file; RFL dashboard; board assurance report. | Diagnostic/screening provider file; abnormal-result pathway; player communication record. | Strong need for provider/control evidence; CQC registration or exemption position must be checked for the provider model. |
| Public/private clinic or paid treatment risk | Full CQC scope decision record; service description; board decision paper; legal/CQC advice file. | Registration application pack if required; complaints; duty of candour; staffing; premises; clinical governance. | High-risk scenario; do not treat as minimum routine pack without specialist advice. |
| Remote triage/advice | Scope decision record; service description; clinical safety-netting process; records/data protection file. | Remote advice/triage assessment; response-time audit; safeguarding/parental consent where academy players are included. | Needs careful factual distinction between ordinary follow-up and a formal remote advice/triage service. |
| Concussion and return-to-play pathway | RFL dashboard; incident record; concussion assurance record; clinical governance meeting record; board assurance report. | HIA reports; GRTP; RTP form; Cognigram/SCAT6; specialist reports. | Strong RFL source basis; board-visible evidence must avoid unnecessary identifiable clinical detail. |
| Contractor-led ambulance/event medical cover | Scope decision record; contractor due diligence file; EAP; pre-match briefing; incident route; board assurance report. | CQC transport/ambulance analysis; command-and-control record; post-event quality report. | Provider/control and transport-to-hospital boundary require validation before relying on contractor status. |

## Scenario 1: Player-only match/training medical service

This is the baseline professional club service: match-day and training-ground care for contracted players and other eligible participants, not a public clinic.

| Evidence requirement | Source or rationale | Usual owner | Visibility | Research uncertainty |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| CQC scope decision record showing participant-only service, sports premises/event context, and no public/private treatment. | CQC treatment guidance includes sports-ground/gym and temporary sporting-event exclusions. | Head of Medical with accountable executive. | Board-visible summary; full rationale retained by governance lead. | Need to validate how associated premises and academy/trialist groups are described for each club. |
| Medical service description defining who is served and what is not provided. | CQC scope depends on facts; statement-of-purpose discipline helps prevent service creep. | Head of Medical. | Board-visible. | Language must avoid implying registration if the club is not registered. |
| RFL standards evidence dashboard for staff, EAP, equipment, records, concussion, medicines, anti-doping, cardiac where applicable. | RFL Medical Standards 2026. | Head of Medical. | Board-visible as compliance status and exceptions. | Exact mandatory/best-practice status by competition still needs checking. |
| Clinical records audit and confidentiality control. | CQC evidence categories and good governance logic; RFL note-keeping requirements. | Head of Medical and data protection lead. | Board-visible as anonymised completeness/exception data; individual records restricted. | Need to confirm current record systems used by clubs. |
| Annual medical governance review. | Regulation 17-style governance discipline and RFL assurance. | Board medical lead or accountable executive. | Board-visible. | Frequency and committee route need validation with club governance calendars. |

Minimum pack placement: **core pack**.

## Scenario 2: External cardiac screening provider

The club commissions an external screening provider to screen players, often because the RFL Medical Standards make cardiac screening mandatory for specified competitions including Championship.

| Evidence requirement | Source or rationale | Usual owner | Visibility | Research uncertainty |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Provider due diligence file, including legal entity, CQC registration or exemption rationale, clinician credentials, indemnity, records, results, and escalation. | CQC diagnostic and screening procedures may apply depending on who performs, analyses, reports, and controls the pathway. | Head of Medical with operations/procurement. | Board-visible as provider status and assurance; contract details may be restricted. | Need provider-specific registration/exemption review. |
| Contract defining clinical control, reporting, abnormal-result pathway, record ownership, confidentiality, incident reporting, and audit rights. | CQC provider/control analysis and contractor governance. | Club operations/procurement with Head of Medical. | Board-visible summary; full contract restricted. | Shared-control models need legal review. |
| Attendance/completion dashboard. | RFL cardiac screening compliance and board assurance. | Head of Medical. | Board-visible as completion percentage and overdue exceptions. | Need to define participation gate if screening incomplete. |
| Abnormal-result pathway and follow-up evidence. | Safe/effective care and diagnostic governance. | Club doctor or screening provider, depending on model. | Board-visible only as anonymised risk/action status; identifiable clinical results restricted. | Need to confirm who communicates results and who owns onward referral. |
| Scope decision record distinguishing club commissioning from club carrying on screening. | CQC scope and provider/control logic. | Accountable executive with Head of Medical. | Board-visible conclusion; detailed legal rationale restricted. | This is the main uncertainty for each provider model. |

Minimum pack placement: **core pack plus contractor/diagnostics appendix**.

## Scenario 3: Public/private clinic or paid treatment risk

This covers a club offering paid physiotherapy, sports medicine, diagnostics, injections, or treatment to the public, sponsors, staff families, schools, or community users outside the participant/event context.

| Evidence requirement | Source or rationale | Usual owner | Visibility | Research uncertainty |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Full CQC scope decision record before launch. | CQC scope guidance and treatment guidance; sports exclusion may not apply to wider public/private services. | Chief executive/accountable executive with Head of Medical. | Board-visible. | High likelihood of needing legal/CQC advice. |
| Service description, user group, payment model, activities, premises, clinicians, records, complaints, incidents, medicines, and provider/control model. | Registration depends on the facts, not the club label. | Project sponsor and Head of Medical. | Board-visible in summary; clinical detail restricted. | The model may shift into independent healthcare provider territory. |
| Board decision record showing approval, risk, advice, and launch conditions. | Good governance and provider accountability. | Board/company secretary. | Board-visible. | Board may need to pause launch until CQC position is settled. |
| Registration readiness pack if required. | CQC registration requirements may include regulated activities, locations, nominated individual, registered manager, policies, and evidence. | Accountable executive. | Board-visible status; application documents controlled. | Specialist regulatory advice needed. |
| Complaints, duty-of-candour, safeguarding, staffing, premises, medicines, and records framework. | Independent healthcare governance pattern. | Registered/provider governance lead if applicable. | Board-visible as assurance; individual cases restricted. | Depends on whether service is launched and under what legal entity. |

Minimum pack placement: **specialist high-risk pack, not routine minimum pack**.

## Scenario 4: Remote triage/advice

This covers a formal remote app, hotline, or advice pathway, especially if it prioritises urgent needs, gives immediate advice, or extends beyond contracted players.

| Evidence requirement | Source or rationale | Usual owner | Visibility | Research uncertainty |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Scope decision record distinguishing ordinary follow-up from a formal remote advice/triage service. | CQC transport, triage, and medical advice provided remotely guidance. | Head of Medical with accountable executive. | Board-visible conclusion. | The boundary is fact-sensitive and needs careful review. |
| Eligibility rules and service description. | User group and activity determine registration risk. | Head of Medical and service owner. | Board-visible. | Academy, parent, community, or public access changes the risk. |
| Clinical safety-netting and escalation process. | Safe care and treatment; remote advice risk management. | Clinical governance lead. | Board-visible as protocol status; individual advice records restricted. | Need clinician validation and possible clinical safety review. |
| Record, response-time, and audit process. | Evidence of safe, consistent remote advice and outcomes. | Head of Medical. | Board-visible as aggregate audit; individual records restricted. | Current systems may not support structured remote advice records. |
| Data protection, consent, safeguarding, and parental consent model where minors are included. | Health data and safeguarding duties. | Data protection lead and safeguarding lead. | Board-visible as assurance; case details restricted. | Child/academy use may require separate pathway. |

Minimum pack placement: **core pack only if limited follow-up; specialist remote-advice appendix if formal service**.

## Scenario 5: Concussion and return-to-play pathway

This covers suspected concussion, HIA, post-match care, GRTP, reporting, return-to-play, loan-player handover, and specialist referral.

| Evidence requirement | Source or rationale | Usual owner | Visibility | Research uncertainty |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Baseline completion dashboard. | RFL concussion requirements for baseline assessments. | Head of Medical. | Board-visible as completion and exceptions. | Need competition-specific baseline requirements confirmed. |
| HIA/concussion report, removal record, and post-injury advice. | RFL Section E and safe care evidence. | Match-day doctor/club doctor. | Individual report restricted; board sees aggregate compliance and exceptions. | Need exact submission evidence and timing by competition. |
| GRTP log and doctor clearance evidence. | RFL GRTP requirements and player safety. | Club doctor. | Restricted clinical record; board-visible as compliant/not compliant and overdue risks. | Need clear rule on board visibility when selection pressure exists. |
| Return-to-play form, Cognigram/SCAT6, specialist reports where required. | RFL reporting and return-to-play controls. | Club doctor. | Restricted; anonymised board exception reporting. | Specialist referral thresholds need validation. |
| Clinical governance review of misses, late reports, symptoms masked by medication, and loan-player notification issues. | CQC learning culture and RFL reporting assurance. | Head of Medical. | Board-visible as themes/actions. | Need alignment with league reporting and parent-club protocols. |

Minimum pack placement: **core pack plus concussion appendix**.

## Scenario 6: Contractor-led ambulance/event medical cover

This covers a club contracting external event medical cover, paramedics, ambulance capability, first aiders, or pitch-side support for home fixtures.

| Evidence requirement | Source or rationale | Usual owner | Visibility | Research uncertainty |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Contractor due diligence file including legal entity, CQC registration or exemption analysis, clinical lead, indemnity, staff credentials, incident route, and quality reports. | CQC provider/control and transport activity guidance. | Operations/procurement with Head of Medical. | Board-visible status; detailed credentials and contract restricted as needed. | Contractor registration may not cover every activity or location. |
| Command-and-control record between club clinicians and contractor. | Provider/control clarity and emergency planning. | Head of Medical and match operations. | Board-visible as approved model. | Shared control is the main risk. |
| Emergency action plan and pre-match briefing record. | RFL EAP and pre-match briefing requirements. | Home club lead clinician and operations. | Board-visible completion status; operational details restricted where needed. | Venue changes and temporary arrangements need review. |
| Equipment, medicines, ambulance, communication, and access checks. | RFL equipment and emergency standards; safe care. | Contractor clinical lead and club match operations. | Board-visible as pass/fail exceptions. | Need clarity on who owns each check. |
| Incident, serious injury, complaint, safeguarding, CQC notification, and post-event report route. | Good governance, CQC/RFL reporting, and contractor assurance. | Head of Medical and contractor lead. | Board-visible themes/actions; identifiable clinical detail restricted. | Need to validate duty-of-candour/CQC route where contractor is registered provider. |

Minimum pack placement: **core pack plus contractor/event-medical appendix**.

## Cross-scenario evidence boundaries

| Evidence type | Board-visible | Restricted clinical/governance record |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Scope decision | Conclusion, date, reviewer, key conditions, triggers for review. | Detailed legal advice, disputed facts, provider-control analysis where sensitive. |
| RFL compliance | Completion status, overdue actions, exceptions, risk rating. | Individual player records, clinical assessments, identifiable health information. |
| Concussion | Compliance rate, overdue GRTP/RTP exceptions, learning actions. | HIA forms, Cognigram/SCAT6 results, clinical notes, specialist reports. |
| Cardiac screening | Provider status, completion rate, abnormal-result pathway assurance. | Individual results, detailed reports, referral records. |
| Medicines/anti-doping | Audit status, incident themes, TUE process assurance. | Player medication records, indications, TUE medical evidence. |
| Contractors | Registration/exemption status, contract assurance, quality report themes. | Full clinical records and provider confidential information unless needed for assurance. |

## Research conclusion for item 2

The minimum pack appears to need two layers:

1. **Core club medical governance pack**: scope decision, service description, RFL dashboard, clinical governance record, incident/complaint route, contractor register, board assurance report, annual review.
2. **Scenario appendices**: diagnostics/cardiac screening, public/private clinic, remote triage/advice, concussion, medicines/anti-doping, contractor/event medical cover, academy/children.

This supports a research conclusion, not a final specification: the pack can be defined only after validating which scenarios are active for the club and which are merely future-risk triggers.
