# Research Scope and Assumptions

This page reframes roadmap item 1. The immediate issue is not "target users and buying groups"; that is too product-shaped. The research first needs to define **audience, decision context, and boundary**.

The working question is: **what is this research trying to make clear, for whom, and for what decision?**

The current scope decision is recorded in [Scope Decision Record](scope-decision-record.md).

## Scope options

| Option | Description | What it would prioritise | Risk if chosen too early |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Single-club governance research | Research focused on one professional Rugby League club, such as a Championship club medical service. | Board briefing, Head of Medical pack, RFL evidence, CQC scope decision, contractor controls. | May not generalise to other clubs or sports. |
| Professional Rugby League framework | Research reusable across Championship, Super League, Women's Super League, academy, reserves, and other professional Rugby League contexts. | Competition-specific RFL evidence, club operating model, league reporting, common scenarios. | May become too broad before one club workflow is understood. |
| Professional sport healthcare governance | Research across Rugby League and comparable professional sports. | Sports-ground exclusions, private healthcare edge cases, sport EMRs, safeguarding, medical governance patterns. | May dilute the Rugby League/RFL specificity. |
| Platform opportunity research | Research a possible online product or service. | Tool landscape, evidence packs, workflow, exports, integrations, vendor gaps. | Risks jumping to solution before requirements are validated. |
| Board assurance pack | Research the immediate board-facing output from Head of Medical. | Plain-English board paper, risk register, evidence pack, decision record, action plan. | May under-research operational and clinical record detail. |

## Current working boundary

Until changed, the wiki should assume this order:

1. **Primary scope**: an online CQC inspection preparation platform for professional sports clubs, using a Championship Rugby League Club as the starting example.
2. **Immediate output**: a guided preparation and assurance knowledge base for board, Head of Medical, clinical, operational, safeguarding/welfare, and contractor-facing understanding.
3. **Secondary output**: reusable research structure that may later support other professional Rugby League clubs and comparable professional sports clubs.
4. **Product/platform angle**: relevant as an online preparation experience, but not yet a full software implementation specification.
5. **Out of scope for now**: full product specification, pricing, sales personas, buying committee design, implementation architecture, and vendor selection.

## Audience and decision context

| Audience | Decision they may need to make | Research they need |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Board/CEO | Is the club exposed to CQC registration or healthcare governance risk? What needs action? | Board briefing, scope conclusion, risk register, assurance pack, unresolved issues. |
| Head of Medical | What evidence is needed to show the service is safe, governed, and RFL/CQC-aware? | Minimum pack, RFL evidence map, clinical governance model, contractor controls. |
| Club operations | Are venues, EAPs, contractors, equipment, and match-day processes evidenced? | Scenario matrix, EAP/equipment/contractor evidence, command-and-control. |
| Clinicians | What records, professional boundaries, and escalation routes protect clinical judgement and player welfare? | Clinical record boundary, medicines/concussion controls, information-sharing rules. |
| Safeguarding/welfare | What overlaps with academy, mental health, disclosure, welfare, and child protection? | Safeguarding and clinical-record separation, welfare escalation, access rules. |
| Legal/CQC adviser | Does the research identify the facts needed for a defensible CQC scope view? | CQC scope question bank, worked examples, contracts, service descriptions. |
| RFL/league source | Are RFL standards interpreted correctly for the competition context? | RFL clause status register and Championship-specific uncertainties. |
| Vendors | Can existing tools support the evidence needs? | Tool capability comparison and export/permission questions. |

## Scope decisions still needed

Before moving into validation interviews or solution design, these questions need answering:

| Question | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Is the concrete reference club Sheffield Eagles, a generic Championship club, Rugby League generally, or a comparable professional sport? | Determines how specific the pack, governing-body references, and RFL status register should be. |
| Is the primary deliverable a board paper, a guided online preparation pathway, or both? | Determines whether pages should optimise for directors, operational medical teams, or first-time CQC learning. |
| Should academy/scholarship care be in the first research phase? | Adds safeguarding, parent/guardian, school, and child welfare complexity. |
| Should the research cover public clinics and paid treatment only as risk triggers, or as possible future services? | Changes the depth of CQC registration research needed. |
| Should platform research remain secondary until after governance scope is validated? | Prevents premature product framing. |

## Recommended next posture

The safest next posture is:

- treat item 1 as **research audience and decision context**, with [Scope Decision Record](scope-decision-record.md) as the current answer;
- keep item 2 as the evidence pack research;
- keep item 3 as RFL status and evidence research;
- keep item 5 as CQC factual decision research;
- keep item 6 as market/tool research only;
- defer item 4 platform information architecture until the scope questions above are settled.
