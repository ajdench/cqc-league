# Assurance Pack Templates

These templates turn the research into evidence a club can use repeatedly. They are intentionally concise so they can be copied into board papers, committee packs, contracts, or internal forms.

## 2. Medical service statement of purpose

| Section | Minimum content |
| --- | --- |
| Service purpose | What the club medical service exists to do. |
| Legal entity | Club company/charity/partnership and address. |
| Aims and objectives | Player welfare, safe participation, emergency care, rehabilitation, compliance with RFL standards. |
| People served | First team, reserves, academy, women's teams, scholarship, match officials, staff, public exclusions. |
| Services provided | Match-day care, training care, injury assessment, rehabilitation, concussion management, emergency care, referrals, screening. |
| Services not provided | Public clinic, paid private treatment, remote urgent triage, IV wellness, diagnostics, or other exclusions unless approved. |
| Locations | Stadium, training ground, academy, away fixtures, temporary events, remote administration. |
| Clinical governance | Board committee, accountable executive, clinical governance lead, head doctor, safeguarding lead, information governance lead. |
| Records and confidentiality | System, access, retention, player consent, coach information boundaries. |
| Incidents and complaints | How concerns, complaints, serious incidents, safeguarding, and duty-of-candour reviews are handled. |
| Contractors | External providers, registration/exemption evidence, audit and reporting. |
| Review | Annual board approval and update after service change. |

## 9. Annual medical governance review

Agenda:

1. Current CQC registration/exclusion assessment.
2. RFL Medical Standards compliance.
3. Clinical governance structure and named leads.
4. Incidents, complaints, safeguarding, and serious injury reports.
5. Concussion and return-to-play audit.
6. Medicines, prescribing, drugs box, anti-doping, and TUE audit.
7. Cardiac screening and diagnostics provider assurance.
8. Records, confidentiality, data sharing, and loan-player handovers.
9. Contractor due diligence and quality reports.
10. Player experience and clinician speaking-up feedback.
11. Facilities, equipment, emergency action plans, and infection control.
12. Risk register and action plan for next season.
