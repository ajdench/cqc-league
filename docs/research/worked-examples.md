# Worked Examples

These examples show how to apply the [Registration Decision Model](registration-decision-model.md), [CQC Registration Edge Cases](cqc-registration-edge-cases.md), and [Assurance Pack Templates](assurance-pack-templates.md) to realistic Rugby League club medical services.

The examples are not legal determinations. They identify the likely posture, the reason, and the evidence a club should hold before relying on an exclusion or launching a new service.

## Summary matrix

| Example | Likely CQC posture | Main risk | Primary control |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Player-only training room | Usually lower risk if solely participant care at associated sports premises | Service creep into staff/public treatment | Statement of purpose and eligibility boundary. |
| Public sports injury clinic | High registration risk | Club becomes independent healthcare provider | CQC scope assessment before launch. |
| Cardiac screening by external provider | Medium risk | Diagnostic provider/control ambiguity | Contract, registration/exemption check, results governance. |
| Away fixture concussion | Lower CQC registration risk, high governance risk | Records, HIA reporting, handover, GRTP control | RFL concussion process and clinical record audit. |
| Club doctor injection or prescribing | Medium to high risk | Medicines, anti-doping, registration boundary | Medicines log, Global DRO/TUE check, clinical indication. |
| Remote injury triage app | Medium to high risk | Formal remote triage/advice service | CQC remote advice assessment and safety-netting. |
| Contractor event medical cover | Medium risk | Unclear provider/control and incident duties | Contractor due diligence and quality reporting. |
| Academy medical pathway | Medium governance risk | Children, consent, safeguarding, confidentiality | Safeguarding, consent, records, parent communication. |

## Example 1: Player-only training room

### Scenario

The club operates a treatment room at its training ground. Club-employed or contracted clinicians assess and treat contracted players, academy players, and trialists for Rugby League injuries. The room is not advertised to the public and does not charge separate treatment fees.

### Likely posture

This is usually lower CQC registration risk if the activity is solely for people taking part in sporting activity and is delivered at a sports ground, gymnasium, or associated premises. The relevant external source is CQC's [Treatment of disease, disorder or injury](https://www.cqc.org.uk/guidance-regulation/providers/registration/scope-registration/regulated-activities/treatment-disease-disorder-or-injury) guidance, which includes sport and first-aid exclusions.

### Why

The activity looks like participant-only sports medical care, not a public healthcare service. However, the club still controls clinicians, records, premises, medicines, referrals, and return-to-play decisions, so it needs a CQC-style governance system even if registration is not required.

### Evidence to hold

- A statement of purpose saying the room is for players/participants only.
- Clinical records and information-governance controls.
- Treatment-room and equipment checks aligned to the [RFL Medical Standards 2026](https://www.rugby-league.com/uploads/docs/Medical%20Standards%202026.pdf).
- Concussion, safeguarding, medicines, infection-control, chaperone, and emergency action policies.
- Annual review showing the room has not drifted into public or paid private care.

### Do before change

If the club wants to treat staff families, sponsors, local schools, community players, or the public, rerun the [CQC scope assessment](assurance-pack-templates.md#1-cqc-scope-assessment).

## Example 2: Public sports injury clinic

### Scenario

The club opens the medical room two evenings per week for paying members of the public with sports injuries. Appointments are promoted on the club website and delivered by the club's doctor and physiotherapists.

### Likely posture

High CQC registration risk. This looks much closer to independent healthcare than participant-only sports treatment. Start with CQC's [scope of registration](https://www.cqc.org.uk/guidance-regulation/providers/registration/scope-registration), [independent healthcare services](https://www.cqc.org.uk/guidance-providers/independent-healthcare/independent-healthcare-services), and [Treatment of disease, disorder or injury](https://www.cqc.org.uk/guidance-regulation/providers/registration/scope-registration/regulated-activities/treatment-disease-disorder-or-injury) guidance.

### Why

The public clinic is not solely for people taking part in or attending a sporting event. The club may be carrying on treatment of disease, disorder or injury, especially where a doctor assesses, prescribes, injects, supervises, or directs treatment.

### Evidence to hold

- Full regulated activity analysis.
- Legal entity and provider/control analysis.
- Statement of purpose.
- Registered manager and nominated individual analysis if registration is needed.
- Policies for complaints, duty of candour, safeguarding, consent, staffing, medicines, records, and premises.

### Do before launch

Do not launch until the club has a documented registration decision and, if required, CQC registration. Use [Private Healthcare Synergies](private-healthcare-synergies.md) to structure the provider model.

## Example 3: Cardiac screening by external provider

### Scenario

The club commissions an external cardiac screening company to run annual screening for first team, academy, and women's squads at the training ground.

### Likely posture

Medium CQC risk. The clinical service may involve [diagnostic and screening procedures](https://www.cqc.org.uk/guidance-regulation/providers/registration/scope-registration/regulated-activities/diagnostic-and-screening-procedures). The key question is who carries on the diagnostic activity and who controls reporting, onward referral, and clinical governance.

### Why

The [RFL Medical Standards 2026](https://www.rugby-league.com/uploads/docs/Medical%20Standards%202026.pdf) make cardiac screening mandatory for some clubs, but RFL compliance does not settle the CQC provider question. If the external provider controls the clinical service and is appropriately registered or exempt, the club's role may be commissioning and governance. If the club controls the screening, reporting, or clinical pathway, the risk shifts back toward the club.

### Evidence to hold

- External provider CQC registration or written exemption rationale.
- Contract defining provider control, clinical responsibility, results reporting, abnormal-result escalation, records, and indemnity.
- Attendance register and completion dashboard.
- Abnormal result pathway and communication record.
- Board assurance that the club has not accidentally become the diagnostic provider.

### Do before renewal

Run the [contractor due diligence checklist](assurance-pack-templates.md#6-contractor-due-diligence-checklist) annually and whenever the provider changes.

## Example 4: Away fixture concussion

### Scenario

A player suffers a suspected concussion during an away fixture where only the home doctor is present. The player is removed, assessed, and returns home under the away club's care.

### Likely posture

Lower CQC registration risk in the match-event context, but high clinical governance risk. Use the RFL [concussion guidance](https://www.rugby-league.com/governance/medical/concussion/concussion-statement) and the concussion section of the [RFL Medical Standards 2026](https://www.rugby-league.com/uploads/docs/Medical%20Standards%202026.pdf).

### Why

The service likely sits inside the sporting-event or participant-care setting. The risk is not public registration. It is handover, reporting, records, medication masking symptoms, return-to-play pressure, and missed specialist referral.

### Evidence to hold

- HIA/concussion report within required RFL timescale.
- Parent-club notification and handover record.
- Player advice sheet and travel/driving advice.
- Medication review.
- GRTP log overseen by a doctor.
- Post-injury Cognigram and return-to-play form where required.
- Specialist review where RFL criteria are met.

### Do before next match

Use the [concussion assurance checklist](assurance-pack-templates.md#8-concussion-assurance-checklist) before the player is eligible for match participation.

## Example 5: Club doctor injection or prescribing

### Scenario

A club doctor prescribes analgesia and administers an injection to a player to treat an acute Rugby League injury during the season.

### Likely posture

Medium to high governance risk. CQC registration depends on whether the treatment is within the sport/participant exclusion and who controls the activity, but medicines, consent, records, professional duties, and anti-doping controls apply regardless. Relevant sources include CQC's [medicines optimisation](https://www.cqc.org.uk/guidance-regulation/providers/assessment/single-assessment-framework/safe/medicines-optimisation), CQC's [Treatment of disease, disorder or injury](https://www.cqc.org.uk/guidance-regulation/providers/registration/scope-registration/regulated-activities/treatment-disease-disorder-or-injury), [UKAD Search Check Apply](https://www.ukad.org.uk/searchcheckapply), and [GMC prescribing guidance](https://www.gmc-uk.org/professional-standards/the-professional-standards/good-practice-in-prescribing-and-managing-medicines-and-devices).

### Why

Prescribing and administering medicines are high-risk healthcare acts. In elite sport, the additional layer is anti-doping status, TUE timing, pressure to play, and whether medication could mask symptoms or worsen risk.

### Evidence to hold

- Clinical indication and assessment.
- Consent, risks, benefits, alternatives, and return-to-play implications.
- Allergy, interaction, contraindication, and current medication check.
- Global DRO result or UKAD advice.
- TUE decision or note that none is required.
- Dose, route, batch where relevant, clinician, time, follow-up.
- Adverse event monitoring and record of player advice.

### Do before repeat use

Review through the medicines lead and include in the [medicines and anti-doping log](assurance-pack-templates.md#7-medicines-and-anti-doping-log). Patterned or repeated use should trigger audit.

## Example 6: Remote injury triage app

### Scenario

The club offers a remote app where academy players, parents, and community partner teams can submit injury symptoms and receive same-day advice from club clinicians.

### Likely posture

Medium to high CQC risk. CQC's [transport, triage and medical advice provided remotely](https://www.cqc.org.uk/guidance-regulation/providers/registration/scope-registration/regulated-activities/regulated-activities/transport-services-triage-and-medical-advice-provided) guidance should be checked before launch.

### Why

Occasional remote follow-up for known club players is different from a formal remote advice or triage service. Risk increases if the body is established for triage, prioritises urgent needs, gives immediate advice, or serves people outside the club participant group.

### Evidence to hold

- Scope assessment distinguishing follow-up from triage service.
- Eligibility rules and age/parental consent model.
- Clinical safety-netting scripts.
- Escalation to GP, NHS 111, emergency department, ambulance, or safeguarding.
- Record system and response-time monitoring.
- Data protection and confidentiality review.

### Do before launch

Run the [CQC scope assessment](assurance-pack-templates.md#1-cqc-scope-assessment) and get specialist advice if the service is available beyond contracted club players.

## Example 7: Contractor event medical cover

### Scenario

The club contracts an event medical provider for home fixtures. The contractor supplies paramedics, ambulance capability, first aiders, and pitch-side support. Club doctors also attend.

### Likely posture

Medium risk. Match-day sports treatment may be excluded, but transport, ambulance movement to hospital, contractor registration, clinical control, incident reporting, and records must be clear. CQC's [transport and remote triage guidance](https://www.cqc.org.uk/guidance-regulation/providers/registration/scope-registration/regulated-activities/regulated-activities/transport-services-triage-and-medical-advice-provided) is relevant where ambulance transport is provided.

### Why

The club may be the organiser, the contractor may be the clinical provider, or responsibilities may be shared. If the contract is vague, the club may not know who owns incidents, complaints, clinical records, safeguarding escalation, duty of candour, or CQC notifications if a regulated activity is carried on.

### Evidence to hold

- Contractor legal entity, CQC registration or exemption analysis.
- Named clinical lead and command structure.
- Emergency action plan and pre-match briefing record.
- Equipment, medicines, ambulance, and communication checks.
- Incident, complaint, safeguarding, and serious injury escalation route.
- Post-event quality report.

### Do before season start

Complete the [contractor due diligence checklist](assurance-pack-templates.md#6-contractor-due-diligence-checklist) and rehearse command-and-control with club clinicians.

## Example 8: Academy medical pathway

### Scenario

The academy medical team assesses and treats under-18 players, shares information with parents, coaches, schools, and external specialists, and manages concussion return-to-learn and return-to-play.

### Likely posture

Medium governance risk. CQC registration may still be lower where care is solely for sports participants at associated premises, but safeguarding, consent, confidentiality, and information sharing require stronger controls.

### Why

Under-18 care introduces child safeguarding, parental responsibility, capacity, confidentiality, chaperone, welfare, school, and family communication issues. The club also needs to manage pressure from selection, contracts, scholarships, and progression routes.

### Evidence to hold

- Safeguarding lead and escalation pathway.
- Parent/guardian and player consent model.
- Confidentiality policy explaining what is shared with coaches and parents.
- Chaperone and intimate-examination process.
- DBS/credential checks for relevant clinicians.
- Return-to-learn and return-to-play communication.
- Academy-specific incident and complaint route.

### Do before pre-season

Include academy care in the annual [medical governance review](assurance-pack-templates.md#9-annual-medical-governance-review) and the [five-domain board dashboard](governance-operating-model.md#five-domain-dashboard).

## How to use these examples

For each new or changed service:

1. Select the closest worked example.
2. Complete the [CQC scope assessment](assurance-pack-templates.md#1-cqc-scope-assessment).
3. Identify the relevant CQC domain and quality statements in [CQC Assurance Domains and Quality Statements](cqc-assurance-domains-quality-statements.md).
4. Add the controls to the [Governance Operating Model](governance-operating-model.md).
5. Record the decision, evidence, owner, and next review date.
